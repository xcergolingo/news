About: Auto-Regressive LLMs

Insights: Auto-Regressive LLMs are useful, particularly as writing aids, particularly for code. They hallucinate too often, have a very primitive understanding of the physical world, and have primitive planning abilities. They have limited working memory and execute a fixed number of computational steps per generated token. Auto-regressive generation is an exponentially-divergent diffusion process, hence not controllable. Errors accumulate, and the probability of correctness decreases exponentially. A solution would require making LLMs non auto-regressive while preserving their fluency.

About: LLMs

Insights: When empirical evidence clashes with a theory, it is the theory that is wrong (or misinterpreted), not the universe. The well-documented initial resistance of many ML theorists to deep learning was due to complete theory-fueled disbelief: this can’t possibly work! AI is a tool that helps us build things that help people. For instance, LLMs might be a better UX to access information – more adaptive, interactive, engaging. That’s great! But it’s still just a tool, and the end goal is still to help people. There are many paths to the same end. As impressive and useful as they are, they are missing essential characteristics to reach Human-Level AI. But the gap will be bridged, perhaps along the directions expressed in this position paper.
